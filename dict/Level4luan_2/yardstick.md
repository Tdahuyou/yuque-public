# 音标

- 英 `/'jɑːdstɪk/`
- 美 `/'jɑrd'stɪk/`

# 词义

n. 码尺
`something that you compare another thing with, in order to judge how good or successful it is`

# 例句

A property slowdown might endanger some projects by this yardstick. It might also hurt the value of the land that local governments have offered as collateral for their borrowings.
从这个尺度上看，房地产的冷却或许会影响到一部分项目，同时还可能损害地方政府作为抵押而提供的土地的价值。

Our guide has spent the journey telling us that the people of Iran should be our yardstick and not the politics.
一路上导游告诉我们，可以用我们的尺度来衡量伊朗人，但不要用政治眼光。
